There's a theory that underperformance in pre-seed and seed investing can sometimes be attributed to "tourists"—privileged individuals with access to capital and networks who invest casually, as a hobby, or simply because they can. Without a true connection to the work, their heart isn't in it, and they underperform. However, most investors have a coherent reason for taking on the extreme personal, temporal, relational, and professional risks of starting a seed fund. Here's ours.

## We do it for each other.

We are a values-aligned, tightly calibrated tribe that genuinely loves each other. After collaborating on various projects with founders, often achieving great outcomes, we realized that our greatest collective skill is helping founders build and succeed. So, we created a fund to do that together, to the best of our ability, for the good of the tribe. Doing our best work for founders and our investors is also a way we care for one another.

## Building is investing.

Our preferred method of problem-solving—building things—is also an effective investment strategy. By helping founders go to market technically and operationally, we gain insights into products, markets, and teams that we wouldn't otherwise discover. We advance the ball for teams in unexpected ways, and we share our tribe's unique style and ethos as an alternative approach to building and being.

## Evolution is beautiful.

We love being in the back seat as teams evolve. The founder/product journey is an insane, fraught path—no one remains the same by the end, regardless of the outcome. Yet, it's also a beautiful journey of discovery and transformation. We love seeing how founders evolve, how we evolve, and how we are able to support each other through through the process. Win our lose, this process is an end it itself.
